Portal 2 Review

One of the games that I will be reviewing is Portal 2. This is one of my personal favorites. After the events of the first game, our main character begins their journey to escape a facility. Throughout the game, you encounter various characters which may help her escape or may hinder her efforts to do so. The main component that makes this game so unique from others is the portal gun. Using the device you are able to, surprisingly, make 2 portals that allow you to move from one place to another. The complexity of each level is one that is very rare to find in games, especially in its consistency. In addition to the complex levels, the storytelling and the characters are some of the most memorable and unique things in modern storytelling. In my opinion, this game is without a doubt, a 10 out of 10.

Majora's Mask Review

There very rarely comes a game as unique as Majora's Mask. Despite being 23 years old now, it remains a timeless classic that defies the constraints of its era. Released in 2000 for the Nintendo 64, Majora's Mask captivated players with its unconventional time-based mechanics, dark atmosphere, and intricate narrative. The game's innovative three-day cycle, where players must navigate the impending apocalypse and manipulate time to save the world of Termina, set it apart from its predecessors and contemporaries. Majora's Mask goes beyond traditional gaming experiences, delving into existential themes of loss, acceptance, and the inevitable passage of time. Its haunting soundtrack, terrifying imagery, and emotionally powerful characters make an immersive experience that is unforgettable Despite the evolution of gaming technology, Majora's Mask endures as a masterpiece, a testament to the power of storytelling and game design that transcends the boundaries of its time. It is absolutely a 10 out of 10, and a game everybody should play at least once in their lives.
